Culture and Traditions of Akwa Ibom

Akwa Ibom is rich in cultural heritage, reflected in its festivals, cuisine, traditional attire, and unique customs. The people take pride in their hospitality and vibrant celebrations that honor their ancestors and community values.

The state hosts numerous traditional festivals, such as the Ndoko Festival celebrated among the Oron people and the Ekpo Masquerade Festival known for its colorful costumes and energetic dances. These festivals bring people together to celebrate unity, harvest, and heritage.

üç≤ Traditional Food

Akwa Ibom is known for its delicious local dishes, including Afang Soup, Editan Soup, and Ukodo (Yam Pepper Soup). These meals are often served with fufu, garri, or pounded yam and enjoyed during family gatherings and festivals.

üëó Traditional Clothing

Traditional attire in Akwa Ibom reflects elegance and identity. Men commonly wear the Etibo or Senator outfit paired with a hat, while women wear beautifully patterned Wrapper and Blouse adorned with coral beads and head ties.
